Air France Flight 4590 (Concorde) accident summary and investigation notes

Date: 25 July 2000
Site: Gonesse, France
Callsign: AF4590
Registration: F-BTSC
Origin: Paris‚ÄìCDG

Air France flight 4590 was a scheduled Concorde service from Paris to New York. Just after reaching takeoff speed (V1), a loud bang was heard from the rear. With the aircraft already above 220 knots, an abort was no longer possible and the crew committed to the initial climb. Engine 1 and 2 fire warnings illuminated; the flight engineer discharged extinguishers and shut both engines down. Despite their efforts, airspeed was insufficient to sustain flight. Approximately three minutes after takeoff, AF4590 impacted a hotel near CDG, tragically killing all 100 passengers and 9 crew, as well as 4 people on the ground.

Occupants & Outcomes

  • Passengers: 100 (fatal)
  • Crew: 9 (fatal)
  • Ground: 4 fatalities, 6 injuries

Investigation

The official investigation was led by the French BEA, with contributions from the NTSB. Investigators found a metal wear strip on runway 26R that did not belong to the Concorde. Records showed a DC-10 departed five minutes earlier; examination of that aircraft (registration N13067) indicated the strip likely detached from its left engine during takeoff. Debris on the runway caused a puncture to Concorde‚Äôs left main gear tire.

BEA experiments showed the tire failure led to high-energy debris striking the left wing, creating a shockwave inside the full fuel tank, which fractured along weaker points. Leaking fuel ignited, and damage to electrical systems prevented landing gear retraction, increasing drag and reducing airspeed. The sequence culminated in an unsurvivable loss of control.

Aftermath

Following the accident, both operators (British Airways and Air France) grounded their Concorde fleets. Modifications were recommended, including kevlar fuel-tank liners, improved electrical protection, and special burst-resistant tires. Although the type later returned to service, the accident ‚Äî combined with economic pressures post-9/11 ‚Äî contributed to Concorde‚Äôs retirement.

A memorial was established at Gonesse to honor the victims.
